B. Ensemble methods are less prone to overfitting than single decision trees

Explanation:

Ensemble methods, such as random forests, combine the predictions from multiple decision trees to improve the robustness and accuracy of the model. The primary advantage of using an ensemble method over a single decision tree is its ability to reduce the risk of overfitting. While a single decision tree can grow deep and complex, fitting very closely to the training data and capturing noise as if it were a signal, this makes it susceptible to overfitting. Overfitting leads to poor generalization to new, unseen data.

Random forests mitigate this risk by creating multiple trees (each trained on a random subset of the training data and features) and aggregating their predictions. This process:

  • Increases the diversity among the trees within the model.

  • Ensures that individual biases or variances from single trees are less likely to dominate the overall model.

  • Typically results in a more general model that performs better on unseen data.

In an AutoML experiment, what are the evaluation metrics automatically calculated for each run when dealing with regression problems?

Explanation

In an AutoML (Automated Machine Learning) experiment, especially when dealing with regression problems, a variety of evaluation metrics are typically calculated for each run to assess the performance of the models. The options you listed are all common metrics used in regression analysis:

  • A. Mean Absolute Error (MAE)

  • B. Coefficient of Determination (R-squared)

  • C. Root Mean Square Error (RMSE)

  • D. Mean Square Error (MSE)

E. All of the above is the correct answer.

Explanation:

  • Mean Absolute Error (MAE) measures the average magnitude of the errors in a set of predictions, without considering their direction. Its the average over the test sample of the absolute differences between prediction and actual observation where all individual differences have equal weight.

  • Coefficient of Determination (R-squared) indicates the proportion of the variance in the dependent variable that is predictable from the independent variables. It provides an indication of goodness of fit and therefore a measure of how well unseen samples are likely to be predicted by the model.

  • Root Mean Square Error (RMSE) is the square root of the average of squared differences between prediction and actual observation. RMSE is a quadratic scoring rule that also measures the average magnitude of the error. Its the square root of the average of squared differences between prediction and actual outcome.

  • Mean Square Error (MSE), like RMSE, measures the average of the squares of the errorsthat is, the average squared difference between the estimated values and the actual value.

AutoML frameworks typically compute multiple metrics to provide a comprehensive view of the model's performance. By evaluating all these metrics, data scientists can get a better understanding of the model's accuracy, consistency, and overall predictive power.
